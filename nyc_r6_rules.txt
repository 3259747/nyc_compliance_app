NEW YORK CITY ZONING RESOLUTION - DISTRICT R6 QUALITY HOUSING RULES

SECTION 23-633: MAXIMUM HEIGHT AND SETBACK REGULATIONS
In R6 Quality Housing districts, the maximum permitted height of a building or other structure shall be 60 feet or 6 stories, whichever is less, above the base plane. Any portion of a building that exceeds this height is a direct compliance violation.

SECTION 23-145: FOR QUALITY HOUSING BUILDINGS
The maximum lot coverage for a residential building in an R6 district shall not exceed 60 percent of the total lot area on an interior lot. On a corner lot, the maximum lot coverage is permitted to scale up to 80 percent. 

SECTION 23-47: MINIMUM REQUIRED REAR YARDS
In R6 districts, a rear yard with a depth of not less than 30 feet shall be provided at every rear lot line of a residential building. For shallow lots, a reduction of 1 foot of yard depth is permitted for every foot the lot depth falls below 100 feet, down to a absolute minimum floor of 20 feet.
---
PARAMETER 4: FLOOR AREA RATIO (FAR)
- R6 Quality Housing Districts inside the Manhattan Core / typical urban areas have a Maximum FAR of 2.20 for residential buildings.
- R6 Quality Housing Districts outside the Manhattan Core (with wide streets) allow an optional Maximum FAR of 2.43 if utilizing specific Quality Housing bulk regulations.
- Calculation Rule: Total Proposed Floor Area divided by the Total Lot Area must not exceed these limits. If it exceeds 2.20 (or 2.43 where explicitly specified for wide streets), it is a FAILED audit.
---
PARAMETER 5: SKY EXPOSURE PLANE
- In R6 districts, the Sky Exposure Plane begins at a height of 60 feet (or 6 stories) above the street line.
- Above this height, the building must set back matching a slope ratio of 5.6 to 1 on a wide street, or 2.7 to 1 on a narrow street. If a building penetrates this theoretical sloping plane without the required setback, it is a FAILED audit.

---
PARAMETER 6: LOT AREA PER DWELLING UNIT (ZONING DENSITY)
- In R6 Quality Housing districts, the minimum lot area required per dwelling unit (apartment) is typically 680 square feet.
- Calculation Rule: Take the Total Lot Area and divide it by the Total Proposed Number of Dwelling Units. If the resulting square footage per unit is less than 680, the building is overcrowded by law and it is a FAILED audit.
